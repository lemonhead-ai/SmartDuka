# 02 · Vision

---

## Mission Statement

To give every child in East Africa — regardless of their connectivity, their school quality, or their family's income — access to a learning experience that knows who they are, speaks their language, and grows with them.

---

## The Big Idea

Smart Duka is not an app with an AI feature. It is an **AI system with a game interface**.

The duka is the delivery mechanism. The agents are the engine. Together they create a localised and personalised education product that makes learning feel like play.

---

## Where We Are Going

### Phase 1 — Kenya (Hackathon → Launch)
- Swahili + English, Kenyan Shillings (KES), Kenyan goods and names.
- Single market, single currency, two languages.
- Target: primary schools in Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu — teacher-champion distribution model.
- Proof of concept: measurable learning outcomes over a 6-week deployment.

### Phase 2 — East Africa (6–18 months post-launch)
The Localization Agent makes regional expansion a configuration task, not a rebuild.

| Country | Language additions | Currency |
|---|---|---|
| Uganda | Luganda | UGX |
| Tanzania | Swahili variants (Tanzanian) | TZS |
| Rwanda | Kinyarwanda | RWF |
| Ethiopia | Amharic | ETB |

Same codebase. Same agents. Same game mechanic. New configuration files per market.

### Phase 3 — Global South (18–36 months post-launch)
The localisation framework scales to any market where:
- A corner-shop equivalent exists in the cultural landscape.
- The learning gap in numeracy, literacy, and financial literacy is documented.
- A distribution partner (government, NGO, or school network) exists.

Target markets include West Africa (Nigeria, Ghana, Senegal), South Asia (India, Bangladesh, Pakistan), and Southeast Asia (Indonesia, Philippines).

## Product Principles

These principles are not aspirational. They are constraints that govern every product decision.

### 1. Accessible learning, by design
The current web experience is designed to be clear, responsive, and welcoming on the phones children and families already use. Dedicated mobile-app support is future roadmap work, not a claim of this release.

### 2. Cultural truth, not cultural decoration
Localisation is not translating English content into Swahili. It is generating content that was conceived in Swahili, with Kenyan goods, Kenyan names, Kenyan prices, and Kenyan scenarios.

The Localization Agent exists specifically to enforce this. A scenario that mentions "a loaf of bread at $2.50" has failed. A scenario that mentions "unga ya sembe at KES 120" is correct.

### 3. The game is the school
We do not believe in learning disguised as a game. We believe the game itself is the learning.

Change-giving is not a maths exercise with a shop theme. It is a real commercial transaction happening inside a game world. The child is not practising maths — they are running a business. The maths is just what running a business requires.

### 4. Adults are partners, not an afterthought
Teachers and parents are the most underutilised asset in EdTech. Smart Duka's Insight Agent exists to make them effective — not to give them dashboards they cannot interpret, but to give them specific, actionable sentences they can act on tomorrow morning.

### 5. Agents must earn their place
Every agent exists because it solves a problem that cannot be solved any other way. The Customer Agent exists because hardcoded scenarios run out and become predictable. The Tutor Agent exists because no teacher can watch 50 children simultaneously. The Insight Agent exists because no teacher has time to analyse gameplay data.

If an agent cannot justify its existence in one sentence, it should not exist.

## What Success Looks Like

### For a child
She opens Smart Duka on a family phone with no wifi. A customer arrives — it is Mama Ngozi, an elderly woman who speaks slowly and checks her change twice. The child reads her shopping list, picks the items, counts out KES 47 in change, and hands it over. Mama Ngozi smiles and leaves. A new mission appears: "The school canteen needs restocking before the lunch rush." The child has been doing maths for 20 minutes and has not thought about it once.

### For a teacher
On Monday morning, a teacher opens her weekly report. It says: "Three students in your class are consistently getting change wrong for amounts above KES 200. They play most on weekend mornings. Suggest drilling change-giving in Tuesday's numeracy session." She does not need to understand data. She needs to understand what to do on Tuesday. She does.

### For a parent
A mother receives a notification: "Amina earned her first profit badge this week. She played 5 days in a row and is improving at reading shopping lists. Keep encouraging her." She does not need to understand EdTech. She needs to know her child is learning. She does.

### For the market
A new country is added to Smart Duka. The engineering effort required is: update the Localization Agent's country configuration file, add currency formatting rules, and review the first batch of generated content for cultural accuracy. No new codebase. No new product team. Two weeks of work, not two years.

## What We Are Not Building

Being clear about scope is as important as being clear about vision.

- We are **not** building a learning management system for schools.
- We are **not** building a teacher tool — teachers benefit from us but are not the primary user.
- We are **not** building a screen-time monitoring product.
- We are **not** competing with Khan Academy on content breadth — we compete on cultural depth and personalisation.
- We are **not** building for the urban middle class who already have acceptable learning outcomes.

---

## The Unfair Advantage

Smart Duka's moat is not the technology — large language models are available to everyone. The moat is:

1. **Cultural depth** — the Localization Agent builds a compound advantage. Every month of operation generates more culturally-grounded training signal that makes the agent better.
2. **Distribution insight** — teacher-champion and faith-community distribution gives us a channel that is invisible to Western EdTech companies.
3. **Gameplay intelligence** — agent-guided customer interactions and learner feedback make the experience more engaging and difficult to replicate.
4. **Age-band breadth** — covering 4–13 in a single coherent product means we can grow with a child rather than lose them at each transition.
