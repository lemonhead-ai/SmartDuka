## Overview

**Smart Duka** is an agentic AI-powered educational game for children aged 4–13 across Kenya and East Africa. Children run a virtual corner shop — a *duka* — where they buy stock, serve AI-generated customers, calculate change, read shopping lists, and manage a simple ledger. Seven specialised GPT-5.6 agents work in the background to personalise every session in real time.

The product is built for the **OpenAI Build Week Hackathon 2026** (Devpost), targeting the **Education track**. It is also aligned with the **Global Education Challenge 2026** by Social Shifters.

---

## The Problem

Sub-Saharan Africa faces a learning crisis that goes beyond school attendance:

- **90%+** of children in Sub-Saharan Africa cannot read or understand a simple text by age 10.
- **Fewer than 1 in 3** primary school children in East Africa can perform basic arithmetic by end of Grade 3.
- **70%+** of Kenyan adults remain outside formal financial systems — a cycle rooted in childhood financial illiteracy.
- **Only 22%** of rural Kenyan households have consistent internet, making cloud-only EdTech solutions irrelevant for most.
- Existing platforms (Duolingo, Khan Academy) use Western cultural contexts, currencies, and scenarios that feel foreign to East African children.
- No existing platform treats **financial literacy** as a first-class subject alongside numeracy and literacy.
- Teachers cannot personalise learning at scale — average class size exceeds 50 students.

The root cause is not lack of content. It is lack of **relevance, accessibility, and personalisation**.

---

## The Insight

Every child in East Africa knows what a *duka* is.

A duka — a small corner shop — is the most universal commercial institution across the region. Children watch transactions, count change, and understand commerce before they can read. Smart Duka uses this as a **structural scaffold**, not a theme. The shop mechanic is how maths, literacy, and financial reasoning are delivered — not a skin on top of a quiz.

---

## Vision

To become the leading AI-powered educational platform for children across the Global South — starting in East Africa, scaling to the world — where every child learns through experiences that reflect their own community.

**Long-term:** A localisation engine so powerful that entering a new market is a configuration task, not a rebuild.

## Target Users

### Primary: Children (Ages 4–13)
The main player. Runs the duka, serves customers, completes missions.

| Age Band | Profile |
|---|---|
| 4–6 | Pre-literate. Visual-only mode. Icon-based interactions. Counting with visible coins. |
| 7–9 | Early reader. Simple addition and subtraction. Swahili/English word matching. |
| 10–13 | Fluent reader. Full ledger mode. Multi-item orders, discounts, stock management. |

### Secondary: Teachers
Champions of the product in school settings. Use the teacher dashboard to monitor classroom progress and receive weekly Insight Agent reports. Do not need data literacy — reports are in plain Swahili and English.

### Tertiary: Parents & Guardians
Receive plain-language weekly summaries of their child's progress via the parent dashboard. Notified on milestones and streaks.

## Core Features

### Student-Facing
- **Duka Simulation** — Run a virtual corner shop. Buy stock, set prices, serve customers, give change, manage daily ledger.
- **AI Customers** — Every customer is generated by the Customer Agent: unique personality, shopping list, budget, and behaviour.
- **Missions** — Daily and weekly narrative quests generated by the Mission Agent ("Help Mama Wanjiku restock before market day").
- **Adaptive Difficulty** — The Difficulty Agent and Tutor Agent silently adjust challenge level based on real-time error patterns.
- **Hints** — The Tutor Agent generates targeted hints after three consecutive identical mistakes. Never intrusive, always contextual.
- **Inventory Management** — Stock runs low, child must reorder from suppliers. Introduces multiplication and budgeting.
- **End-of-Day Ledger** — Child tallies income vs expenses. Intro to profit/loss.
- **Rewards** — Coins, badges, shop upgrades, avatar items, story unlocks — personalised by the Reward Agent.
- **Streaks** — Daily play streak tracking with visual motivation.
- **Leaderboard** — Class and national boards. Teachers can scope to their classroom.
- **Avatar & Profile** — Custom avatar, shop name, and personalised profile.
- **Offline Play** — Full gameplay available offline. Agents resync when connectivity returns.
- **Localisation** — Swahili and English toggle. All content uses KES, local goods, local names.

### Parent Dashboard
- Weekly Insight Agent report in plain language.
- Child's current streak, coins earned, skills being practised.
- Milestone notifications (first profit, 10-day streak, etc.).
- PIN-protected child account management.

### Teacher Dashboard
- Class-level overview: which students are struggling, which are excelling.
- Per-student Insight Agent reports.
- Classroom leaderboard scope.
- Lesson suggestions based on class-wide error patterns (generated by Insight Agent).

## AI Agent System

Seven specialised GPT-5.6 Sol agents power the intelligence layer.

| # | Agent | Role |
|---|---|---|
| 01 | **Customer Agent** | Generates NPC customers with personalities, shopping lists, budgets, and behaviours. Pre-generates 20–30 scenarios per sync for offline use. |
| 02 | **Tutor Agent** | Analyses error patterns per child. Generates targeted hints. Adjusts scenario selection to drill weak areas. |
| 03 | **Mission Agent** | Generates daily and weekly narrative quests scoped to current skill ceiling. Creates the story progression layer. |
| 04 | **Localization Agent** | Ensures all content uses local goods (unga, sukari, mandazi), KES denominations, Swahili patterns, culturally accurate scenarios. Handles multi-country expansion. |
| 05 | **Insight Agent** | Generates plain-language weekly reports for teachers and guardians. Produces classroom-level summaries. |
| 06 | **Difficulty Agent** | Calibrates macro difficulty tier per child per session. Maintains each child in their optimal learning zone. Outputs a difficulty profile read by all other agents. |
| 07 | **Reward Agent** | Determines personalised reward drops based on what each child responds to most (social vs personal rewards). Learns preference over time. |

All agents:
- Are orchestrated by the sync service, not called directly from routes.
- Use `gpt-5.6` exclusively.
- Load system prompts from `/backend/src/prompts/*.md` at runtime.
- Return structured JSON matching a Pydantic schema.
- Never block gameplay — pre-generate and cache content for offline use.

## Architecture Principles

- **Offline-first** — Core gameplay works with zero internet. Agents enhance, never gatekeep.
- **Agent-orchestrated** — No hardcoded content. All scenarios, missions, hints, and reports are AI-generated.
- **Clean architecture** — Separation of concerns: routes → services → repositories → database.
- **Modular** — Each agent, feature, and API domain is independently deployable and testable.
- **Scalable** — Localization Agent makes any new market a config task. Database schema supports multi-country.
- **Secure** — JWT auth, PIN-based child accounts, no PII in gameplay logs.

## Success Criteria (Hackathon Demo)

A complete end-to-end run that demonstrates:

1. Child signs in with PIN and lands on their personalised dashboard.
2. Active mission is displayed — generated by the Mission Agent.
3. Child enters the shop. A live AI customer arrives (Customer Agent, GPT-5.6, real-time).
4. Child reads the shopping list, selects items, calculates the total, gives change.
5. After two transactions, the Tutor Agent visibly adjusts the next customer's complexity.
6. Child completes the mission and receives a personalised reward (Reward Agent).
7. Demo of offline mode: wifi disabled, game continues without interruption from cache.
8. Wifi re-enabled: sync happens silently in the background, new scenarios loaded.
9. Parent/teacher switches to the parents screen and sees Amina's Insight Agent report.
10. Language toggle between English and Swahili — all content switches, proving localisation is real.
